Ramadan Mubarak!

In this trying time, I begin by praying for your safety, for your families, our community, and all humanity – may Allah lift this tribulation, grant us all patience and steadfastness, and cause it to be a source of nearness to Him. Further, I wish to sincerely appreciate the great honor of serving you alongside our incredible staff and dedicated volunteers. At a time when we are all consumed with the impact of the COVID-19 (coronavirus) pandemic on our lives, you have never wavered in supporting our beloved Islamic Association of Raleigh with your time, donations, contributions, and duaa. May Allah accept in your scales and make it a source of continuous good for you and your families, ameen.

Even as the pandemic forced our building to close, your masjid has kept leading, serving, and giving. The difficult but necessary step of building closure was taken alongside a dozen masajid throughout the region, and our joint Triangle Imams Council Declaration was referenced across the Triangle region, in scholarly circles, and even internationally as a model of unity, solidarity, and togetherness alhamdulillah.

As we wish you Ramadan Mubarak, we are committed to being with you every step of the way - Keeping You Connected. The first day of fasting will be Friday, April 24, 2020. Download our Ramadan timetable/imsakiyya to track iftar and suhur times daily, integrate our Google Calendar and download our IAR App on Android and iPhone to get automated notifications at prayers. For those unable to fast due to chronic conditions, you may offer the Fidya of $7 per day of missed fast, used exclusively to feed those in need. Under the leadership of Imam Muamar Dahnoun, a number of religious rulings related to the current circumstances are posted in English and Arabic for your benefit. After reviewing our online answers, if you have additional questions please visit our Imam’s Corner, click “Religious Inquiries” and we will be happy to assist.

Even as our doors are closed, we are offering the most extensive lineup of Ramadan programming in IAR history to help you experience energizing worship, community solidarity, and a vibrant connection with Allah (swt). We encourage you to orient your day with us. After iftar each day, join us in our “100 Taraweeh, 500 Khatma” program. If you can comfortably read from the Quran in Arabic, join your family in offering 2, 4, or more raka’at of Taraweeh in your home after Isha prayers with any part of the Quran. Or, each night in Ramadan you can enliven your home with the Quran by tuning in to a livecast of taraweeh offered our esteemed Sh. Mohamed Badawy, Sh. Hoosain Dalvi, along with huffaz graduates from An-Noor Quran Academy. (This will not serve as salah, but as listening & studying the Quran from our homes). Every night at 10:30pm, whether you’re praying at home or joining us on the webcast, join us for our nightly khatera program sharing inspiring stories of strength in hard times from our tradition. Each week, gather your family at 1:30pm on Friday to watch our Jumuah Reminder and connect with your community. To receive a full calendar of our many programs, from a Ramadan planning workshop to daily spiritual duaa & dhikr, sign up for our email list to receive a downloadable calendar later this week.

Our extensive lineup of Ramadan programs expands upon a full month of Sha’ban programs, Keeping You Connected with Allah, with your deen, and with each other. If you missed any of these programs, you can still access the recordings - and share them with your family and friends. We hosted a number of special guests including internationally renowned scholars Shaykh Dr. Ahmed Aissa al-Ma’sarawi, Head Reciter and Chairman of the Quran Review Committee Emeritus of Egypt; Shaykh Dr. Salah al-Sawy, the Secretary General for the Assembly of Muslim Jurists in America (AMJA) and President of Mishkah University; recognized reciter Sheikh Ramadan Elsabagh; and many others. Our nightly interactive livestream, Left My Heart at the Masjid, featured our Mental Health team discussing anxiety and depression, Imam Muamar on how janazas will be adjusted, physicians discussing Coronavirus, and our school principals and chairmen. Our classes, halaqa circles, and youth programs transitioned online as well.

Your masjid is also on the front lines of helping those facing hardship in this time. IAR has actively worked during this period with many activists from our community and relief agencies to help ease the hardship of these difficult times. Your contributions helped facilitate a drive-thru food drive, providing essential food staples for area families; and are enabling the upcoming Ramadan drive-thru food pantry as well. Financial assistance for those qualified is available via the website, by email, or by postal mail to IAR, 808 Atwater St, Raleigh, NC 27607.

Al-Ma’idah Cafe is here to help you enjoy a delicious Ramadan iftar, or to sponsor a meal for those in need. Please visit our new online ordering portal to bring the flavors from our kitchen to your home. Every purchase you make from Al-Ma’idah goes directly towards supporting your masjid.

This essential work is only possible through your generous donations and continued support, now more than ever. To that end, I invite you to mark your calendar for our virtual Ramadan fundraiser on Friday, May 1st. In addition we are rolling out new ways to contribute: to keep up your habit of consistent sadaqa, you can text IAR1 followed by a dollar amount to 73256 and donate just like our Jumuah collection boxes! We will also offer a similar collection system for Zakat Ul-Fitr obligations - more details to follow.

I am truly grateful to serve alongside so many who have worked tirelessly to see our community through this trying time. I cannot put in words the immense contributions of our staff and volunteers, from our Safety and Security securing our building and directing flow during our essential services programs, to Social & Welfare ensuring food and financial assistance reach those in need, to Facilities Maintenance making critical repairs and IT upgrades to enable us to serve, to Youth who help foster space to converse, learn, and support one another, to Al-Ma’idah helping take a load off our families and bring meals to families facing hardship, to Finance, Fundraising, Administration for revisiting our strategy and helping ensure our essential work continues, to our Outreach and Advocates teams who welcomed a new shahada and continue our work standing up for our principles, to others too many to mention. I would like to also acknowledge the efforts of Br. Kashif Osman, who has done an exceptional job preparing a new marketing and communications strategy to stay in touch with all of you, and I would like to thank our Shura, Administration and Office of the Imam for their ongoing guidance and leadership during this trying time. Alhamdulillah, these efforts have been acknowledged by news outlets such as the News and Observer and Spectrum News, and are appreciated by countless words and duaas by those whom you serve.
